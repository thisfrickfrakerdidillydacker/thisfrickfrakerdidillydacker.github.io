Between the Bars Prison Culture Poetry Power Poetry
Your stone goddess stands so tall,
Her eyes bound
Claiming blindness,
And yet she sees
Ever so clearly
Ones sins,
As if they were written in blood on skin
Of which she knows the color

No, they are bound to hide
The truth,
The devil's eyes
They see sins not as god does
Not as falls to be overcome
Not as tragedies to be freed from
But as weights, to be tied to sinners and drag them to hell
Pray to god or his absence that those eyes turn not upon your soul,
For who could withstand the gaze of eternity?
Certainly not you
