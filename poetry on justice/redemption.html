Redemption:



They sit behind bars
And say they’ve been given redemption
They say it hurts, it’s hard
But they’re glad they came here
They’ll be better for this time
When they leave they’ll get a job
Beat their addictions
But too often, too soon, once more,
They sit behind bars
and say they’ve been given redemption



Authors note:



This poen was inspired by the book "I refuse for the devil to take my soul" by Lili Kobielski. It is a compolation of portrat photos of prisoners and interviews on thier situations and lives leading up to their imprisonment. While reading I was struck by thier hope, most said they would ever come back once they got out, and not one said they thought they would return, even though statistacly, more than half of them would. So I wrote a poem about that descrepency and how their hope is betrayed.
